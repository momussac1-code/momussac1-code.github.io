UK Locksmith Prices 2026 — What Should You Pay?

Published May 2026 — 6 min read

Locksmith pricing in the UK varies widely — from £45 for a simple key cutting to £150+ for an emergency callout at 3am. Here's what you should expect to pay for common locksmith services in 2026.

Common Locksmith Service Prices

Emergency Callout (Daytime)

£50-£90 — Most reputable locksmiths charge between £50 and £90 for a standard daytime emergency callout. This usually includes getting you back into your property if you're locked out.

Emergency Callout (Night/Weekend)

£80-£150 — Out-of-hours callouts (after 6pm, weekends, bank holidays) typically cost more. Be suspicious of anyone charging over £150 for a standard lockout.

Lock Change (Standard Euro Cylinder)

£35-£65 — Replacing a standard euro cylinder lock, including parts and labour. Anti-snap cylinders cost more (£45-£80).

uPVC Door Lock Repair

£60-£120 — uPVC door mechanism repairs vary depending on the fault. Simple adjustments cost less; full mechanism replacements cost more.

Key Cutting

£2-£10 — Standard key cutting is cheap. High-security keys, car keys, and remote fobs cost significantly more.

Car Key Replacement

£80-£250+ — Car key replacement costs vary massively depending on the make and model. A basic Ford key may cost £80-100; a BMW key with remote fob programming can cost £200-300+.

What to Watch Out For

  • "No call-out fee" ads — Some locksmiths advertise no call-out fee but inflate the work cost. Always get a total price before they start.
  • Cash-only requests — Legitimate locksmiths accept card payments. Cash-only is a common scam indicator.
  • Pressure to replace — If a locksmith tells you your lock is "unsafe" or "illegal" without explanation, get a second opinion.

How to Get a Fair Price

  1. Call 2-3 locksmiths and compare quotes over the phone
  2. Ask for a fixed price, not an estimate
  3. Confirm if VAT is included in the quoted price
