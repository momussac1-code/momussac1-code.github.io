10 Burglary Prevention Tips Every UK Homeowner Should Know

Published May 2026 — 6 min read

Most burglaries in the UK are opportunistic. Burglars look for easy targets — homes with weak locks, no visible security, or signs that the owners are away. Here are 10 things you can do to make your home a harder target.

1. Upgrade to BS3621-Approved Locks

The British Standard for door locks is BS3621. If your existing locks don't meet this standard, a locksmith can upgrade them. It's also often required by home insurance policies.

2. Install a Letterbox Deflector

Many burglars use the letterbox to reach in and unlock doors. A letterbox deflector plate or cage blocks this access point. Cost: £10-20 on Amazon.

3. Use Timer Switches for Lights

When you're out, make the house look occupied. Plug-in timer switches cost £5-10 and can turn lights, radios, or TVs on and off at set times.

4. Secure Your Back Garden

Burglars often access properties through the back garden. Secure side gates with a padlock (not just a latch) and keep ladders and tools locked away.

5. Install Motion Sensor Lights

A sudden light is one of the best deterrents. Motion-activated floodlights cost £15-40 and are easy to install. Position them over back and side doors.

6. Lock UPVC Doors Properly

Many people just lift the handle without turning the key. Always lift the handle and lock with the key — this engages the multi-point locking system properly.

7. Get a Smart Doorbell

Ring-style doorbell cameras are proven to reduce package theft and doorstep crime. They also record anyone approaching your front door. Cost: £80-200.

8. Join or Start a Neighbourhood Watch

Active Neighbourhood Watch schemes significantly reduce crime in participating areas. Check if your street has one or start a new group.

9. Don't Advertise Your Absence

Don't post holiday photos on social media while you're away. Wait until you're home. Also, cancel milk and newspaper deliveries if you're going away.

10. Get a Free Security Survey
