uPVC Door Lock Repair — A Complete Guide

Published May 2026 — 8 min read

uPVC door lock problems are one of the most common callouts for locksmiths in the UK. A stuck handle, a key that won't turn, or a mechanism that's failed can leave you locked in or out of your home.

Common uPVC Door Lock Problems

Stiff or Sticky Key

A key that's hard to insert or turn is often caused by a misaligned lock cylinder or dirt inside the mechanism. Try graphite powder (not WD-40, which attracts dirt) to lubricate the cylinder.

Door Handle Drooping or Loose

A drooping handle usually means the internal spring mechanism has failed or the fixings have worked loose. This is a common issue that a locksmith can fix in 20-30 minutes.

Lock Mechanism Won't Engage

If the handle lifts but the lock doesn't fully engage, the gearbox mechanism inside the door is likely worn or broken. This typically requires a new mechanism.

Key Snapped in Lock

A broken key in the lock is a genuine emergency. Don't try to remove it yourself with pliers — you risk damaging the lock further. Call a locksmith who can extract the broken key safely.

Average uPVC Door Lock Repair Costs

  • Cylinder replacement: £35-£65 (including parts)
  • Gearbox / mechanism replacement: £60-£120 (including parts)
  • Handle replacement: £30-£60 (including parts)
  • Broken key extraction: £50-£90 (plus any replacement)

When to Call a Locksmith vs DIY

Call a locksmith if: The lock is completely jammed, a key is broken inside, or the mechanism needs replacing. uPVC door repairs require specialist tools and expertise.

DIY if: The handle just needs tightening (screws visible), or the key needs lubricating with graphite powder.

Preventive Maintenance Tips

  • Lubricate your lock cylinder with graphite powder every 6 months
  • Tighten visible handle screws if they work loose
  • If the key starts to feel stiff, address it early before it becomes a full failure
  • Consider upgrading to anti-snap cylinders for better security
