Home CCTV Installation Guide for UK Homeowners

Published May 2026 — 9 min read

CCTV is one of the most effective deterrents against burglary. A 2019 study found that homes with visible CCTV cameras are significantly less likely to be targeted by burglars.

Types of CCTV Systems

Wired CCTV

Traditional wired systems connect cameras to a central recorder via cables. They're reliable, have no signal interference, but require professional installation.

Wireless CCTV

Wireless cameras connect to your Wi-Fi network. They're easier to install yourself and many come with phone apps for remote viewing. Battery life varies from 3-12 months depending on usage.

Smart Doorbell Cameras

Devices like Ring and Nest Doorbell have become hugely popular in the UK. They're affordable, easy to install, and provide video of anyone approaching your front door.

Estimated Costs

  • Smart doorbell camera: £80-£200
  • Single wireless camera: £60-£300
  • 4-camera wired system (installed): £400-£1,200
  • 8-camera system (installed): £800-£2,000

UK Legal Requirements

Under UK law, your CCTV system must:

  • Not record areas beyond your property boundary (e.g., public pavement, neighbour's garden)
  • Display signs indicating CCTV is in operation
  • Comply with GDPR if recording public areas
  • Store footage securely and delete it after a reasonable period (typically 30 days)

Installation Tips

  • Position cameras at least 2.5m high to prevent tampering
  • Cover all entry points — front door, back door, ground-floor windows
  • Make cameras visible (they work as a deterrent, not just evidence)
  • Consider night vision capability for 24-hour coverage
