The Ultimate Home Security Audit Checklist

Published May 2026 — 10 min read

Most burglaries happen because homeowners overlook simple security weak points. This room-by-room checklist will help you identify and fix vulnerabilities in your home security.

Front of House

  • Is your front door fitted with a BS3621-approved deadlock?
  • Are your door hinges visible and secure (not on the outside)?
  • Do you have a door viewer (peephole) installed?
  • Is your letterbox restrictor fitted (prevents access to locks through letterbox)?
  • Are outdoor lights working and on a timer or sensor?

Windows and Back Doors

  • Are ground-floor windows fitted with key-operated locks?
  • Are patio doors secured with anti-lift devices?
  • Do you have window sensors or alarms?
  • Is the back door locked even when you're home?
  • Are shed and garage doors secured with proper padlocks?

Garden and External

  • Is your garden fence in good condition?
  • Are there hiding spots near doors (overgrown bushes)?
  • Is your ladder locked away (burglars use ladders to access upper floors)?
  • Are valuables like bikes locked and out of sight?

Smart Security Upgrades Worth Considering

If your audit reveals weak spots, consider these upgrades:

  • Smart doorbell camera (Ring, Nest) — deters 50% of would-be burglars
  • Smart lights — programmable to mimic occupancy
  • Window sensors — alert you when a window is opened
  • Anti-snap locks — prevents the most common lock-breaking technique
