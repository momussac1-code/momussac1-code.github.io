How to Choose a Trusted Locksmith in the UK

Published May 2026 — 7 min read

When you're locked out of your home at midnight, it's easy to panic and call the first number you find on Google. Unfortunately, that's exactly what locksmith scammers rely on. Here's how to find a trustworthy locksmith in the UK.

The Problem With Locksmith Scams

Unregulated locksmiths often operate by ranking high in Google ads, arriving on scene, then inflating prices dramatically. Common tactics include claiming the lock is "unsafe" and needs replacing at 3x the market price, or charging hidden call-out fees.

5 Ways to Spot a Genuine Locksmith

1. Check Google Reviews — But Look Closely

A legitimate locksmith should have multiple recent Google reviews. Be wary of businesses with only 5-star reviews and no lower ratings — these may be fake. Look for detailed reviews that mention specific services and locations.

2. Verify Their Physical Address

Genuine locksmiths have a verifiable business address. If their Google Maps pin is in a residential area or their "address" is actually a virtual office, be cautious.

3. Ask for a Price Before Work Starts

A reputable locksmith will provide a clear estimate before beginning any work. If they're vague about pricing or refuse to give a quote over the phone, call someone else.

4. Check for Trade Body Membership

Look for membership in the Master Locksmiths Association (MLA) or similar accredited bodies. While not all good locksmiths are MLA members (many excellent independent locksmiths choose not to join), MLA membership adds credibility.

5. They Arrive in a Marked Vehicle

A professional locksmith will arrive in a branded vehicle. If they turn up in an unmarked car and can't produce ID on request, that's a red flag.

Questions to Ask When You Call

  • Can you give me a fixed price before you start?
  • Do you charge a call-out fee? (Many reputable locksmiths don't)
  • Are you DBS checked?
  • Can I see your ID when you arrive?
  • What payment methods do you accept?
