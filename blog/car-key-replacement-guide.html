Lost Your Car Keys? A Complete Replacement Guide

Published May 2026 — 7 min read

Losing your car keys is frustrating and potentially expensive. The good news: a mobile auto locksmith in Leeds can usually help you on the same day, often within an hour.

Types of Car Keys and Costs

Traditional Metal Key

£5-£20 — Older cars (pre-2000) use simple metal keys. Any locksmith can cut a replacement cheaply and quickly.

Transponder Key

£80-£160 — Most cars from 2000 onwards use transponder keys with a microchip. The locksmith needs to program the chip to your car's immobiliser system.

Remote Fob Key

£120-£250 — Keys with remote locking buttons require programming for both the immobiliser and the remote functions. More complex for premium brands like BMW, Audi, and Mercedes.

Keyless Entry / Smart Key

£200-£400+ — Modern keyless entry systems use proximity keys that communicate with the car's computer. These are the most expensive to replace and require specialist equipment.

How a Mobile Auto Locksmith Helps

A mobile locksmith comes to you — whether you're at home, at work, or stranded at a supermarket car park. They carry equipment to cut and program keys on-site for most vehicle makes.

What to Do If You Lose Your Keys

  1. Check everywhere — bags, coat pockets, between car seats
  2. If you have a spare key, use it to get home or to a safe location
  3. If you don't have a spare, call a mobile auto locksmith
  4. Provide your vehicle registration, make, model, and year
  5. Ask for a fixed price before work starts
  6. Keep the new key safe and consider making a spare immediately
